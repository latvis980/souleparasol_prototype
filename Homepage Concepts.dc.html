Concept 01

The Summer
Collection

A homepage that behaves like a summer you almost remember.

The page is an album of summer moments in the house's aesthetic — texture, light, salt on skin, beautiful objects. Each image sits inside its own frame, the frames suspended over one continuous surface: sand, raw cotton, sun-bleached linen. Nothing is staged. Everything feels found. Among the found moments, five frames are films — the house's mood reels, each finishing the line when summer is… (bare, and four still to come).

Concept 01 · How it moves

The page responds
to presence

The cursor stirs the surface — a soft distortion of the background, or a house motif that trails the hand.

Most frames breathe quietly in place. But five are films. Reach one and it lifts out of the album, expands to fill the screen, and plays — sound and all. On a phone, a quiet cue asks you to turn it sideways; on desktop it simply opens larger.

The texture beneath stays constant — motion reads like weather moving across one surface, not effects switching on and off.

Concept 02

The Disappearing
Guides

We don't talk about the perfume. We hand you the summer it came from.

Five films, five destinations. Each of the house’s five summers is a short film, paired one-to-one with the place it was shot — Pantelleria, Comporta, Portofino, Antiparos, Patmos. A screen opens as its film — when summer is… — and when you watch it through, the reel dissolves into that destination and a short, secret guide to it: the hotel with no sign, the restaurant you have to be told about, the beach no map marks.

Concept 03

The Missing
Word

The homepage is one unfinished sentence.

The most product-forward concept of the set. The page opens almost blank. As you tap, sound and colour bloom under your finger and one line surfaces — when summer is… Words rise and fade: volcano, green, bare. Five of them are keys. Find the right word, and its film begins.

Concept 03 · How it opens

Tap, and the
words rise

The site opens as a blank field — almost nothing. A quiet tip invites you to turn the phone sideways and touch the screen. Every tap answers: a soft note, a bloom of light.

The line appears — when summer is… — and words surface one at a time, then fade. Tapping is the whole pleasure. Before you know it, you have slowed down.

Concept 03 · The hidden keys

Find the word,
and it plays

Seconds in, a second line surfaces: find the right word — and disappear with us. Five of the drifting words are keys, one for each fragrance. Land on a key and it stops fading.

It holds, steadies, and the horizontal film opens full-screen. Forty seconds of sea and sound. A small mystery, solved by hand.

Concept 03 · Why it fits

The word is the door

This turns the house's founding idea — the art of disappearance — into the way the site behaves. Meaning is never handed over; it is found, one word at a time. The fragrance is never explained, only unlocked. And the email arrives naturally: to keep the summer you uncovered, and to be told when it can be worn.
