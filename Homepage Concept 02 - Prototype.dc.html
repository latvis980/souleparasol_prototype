The guide · Le Blanc Froissé

Comporta

when summer is bare

Fabric, skin, salt — and the flattest light in the Mediterranean. What follows are the addresses we only give to people we like. No stars. No signs. Ten places, and the reasons they matter to the house.

The addresses
01
Sal
Table · on the sand

Lunch at Pego with your feet in it. Order the grilled fish and nothing else. Stay until the light goes long.

02
Cavalariça
Dinner · old stable

A converted stable in the village. The kind of room that makes you speak more quietly than you meant to.

03
Casa na Areia
A house · no sign

You won't find it looking. Ask the right person and you'll be told. Sand floors, cork walls, silence.

04
Praia do Pego
Beach · the far end

Walk past the last umbrella, then keep walking. The house was named for what happens after that.

05
Ilha do Arroz
Table · come by boat

A dock restaurant in the rice channels. Arrive by water in the afternoon; leave when it's dark.

06
Museu do Arroz
Room · rice, and quiet

Not a museum, really. A warm room over the water where the rice that made this place is still cooked well.

07
The N261 at dusk
Drive · slowly

Umbrella pines, then rice fields, then nothing. Windows down. This is where the note for Blanc Froissé began.

08
Cais Palafítico
Walk · the wooden pier

The old stilted pier at Carrasqueira. Go at low tide, near six, when the boats lean into the mud.

"Remember that restaurant on the mountain in A Bigger Splash? Like that."
— How we describe Comporta to a friend
